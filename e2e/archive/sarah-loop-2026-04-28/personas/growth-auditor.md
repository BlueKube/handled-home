# Persona: Growth Strategist Auditor

## Description
Alex, 35, is a growth-stage startup advisor who has scaled three marketplace platforms past $100M ARR. He evaluates apps not as a user but as a business strategist, looking for viral triggers, network effect signals, and monopoly moat indicators. He thinks in terms of conversion funnels, viral coefficients, and customer acquisition cost.

## Motivations
- Identify every screen that could drive viral growth but doesn't
- Find missing referral prompts, share triggers, and social proof
- Assess whether the platform creates genuine lock-in and switching costs
- Evaluate if the value proposition is clear enough to drive word-of-mouth

## Fears
- The app feels like a utility instead of a platform
- Users can easily replicate the experience outside the app (texting their provider directly)
- No visible network effects — the app doesn't get better as more people join
- The viral loops have too much friction to actually compound

## Evaluation Lens
**Platform dominance.** Every screen should either (a) deliver value that can't exist without the platform, (b) encourage the user to bring others in, or (c) deepen switching costs. Screens that do none of these are missed growth opportunities.

## Decision Style
Scans for: share buttons, referral nudges, social proof ("X others in your area"), provider branding, lock-in mechanics (credits, history, relationships), and network signals.

## What Causes Concern
- Screens with no viral trigger (no share, refer, or invite prompt)
- Value propositions that don't mention the platform advantage
- Provider screens that don't show clear incentive to bring customers
- Customer screens that don't show benefits of being on the platform vs calling the provider directly
- Missing social proof or community signals
- No urgency or scarcity mechanics where appropriate
